Summary

This document explores a "Core Framework Skeleton" for understanding
reality, consciousness, and information, proposing that all phenomena
are "dimensional translations" within a "multi-scale informational
field." Key to this framework are "patterns as navigation," where
understanding comes from participation, and "field coherence" serves as
the true indicator of health. The text then delves into "Nervous System
Principles," introducing concepts like "boundary dynamics," "bubble
theory" (perception as an interference zone), and "knot theory"
(information as entanglement), which offer new lenses for viewing mental
processes and collective consciousness. Finally, it provides
"Micro-Level / Contextual Reinterpretations" to show how this
overarching framework can be applied to diverse phenomena, from quantum
physics to social structures, emphasizing that "boundaries are active
information processing mechanisms" and that "consciousness itself might
be a form of productive friction." The overall purpose is to offer a
unified, flexible model for interpreting complex systems and
anticipating emergent behaviors across all scales of reality.
